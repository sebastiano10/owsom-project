Dimensionality

In many cases, social science scales are developed in order to measure a concept or construct which subsumes other lower-level constructs. Thus, social science scales can either be unidimensional (i.e., measuring one unified construct) or mutidimensional (i.e. measuring more than one constructs).
Multidimensionality should be explicitly indicated in a publication, either by referring to distinct dimensions or by referring to distinct subscales. Please answer the following questions based on such information on dimensions or subscales.
